Modeling Just Noticeable Differences in Charts VIS'21

Min Lu, Joel Lanir, Chufeng Wang, Yucong Yao, Wen Zhang, Oliver Deussen and Hui Huang
Paper (2.1 MB) | Open Sourced Experiment Data | My Talk at VIS 2021

It is often difficult to compare graphical elements encoding a small difference in value, such as the heights of similar bars in bar chart or angles of similar sections in pie chart. In this work, we model the perception of Just Noticeable Differences (JNDs) in charts with two variables (intensity and distance) and find that JND grows as the exponential function.

Exploring Visual Information Flows in Infographics CHI'20

Min Lu, Chufeng Wang, Joel Lanir, Nanxuan Zhao, Hanspeter Pfister, Daniel Cohen-Or and Hui Huang
Paper (2.0 MB) | InfoVIF dataset | Demo Video (MP4, 21.1 MB) | Online VIF Explorer | My Talk at CHI 2020

We extract Visual Information Flow (VIF), the underlying semantic structure that links graphical elements to convey the information, from a wide variety of infographics designs with various artistic decorations. Using the extracted VIFs of thousands of infographics, we are able to characterize the design space and present a taxonomy of VIF patterns.


Winglets: Visualizing Association with Uncertainty in Multi-class Scatterplots VIS'19

Min Lu, Shuaiqi Wang, Joel Lanir, Noa Fish, Yang Yue, Daniel Cohen-Or and Hui Huang
Paper (2.7 MB) | Source Code (Python) | My Talk at VIS 2019

Winglets, are proposed as an enhancement to better perceptually pronounce multiple classes in the classic scatterplot. Designed as a pair of dual-sided strokes belonging to a data point, Winglets leverage the Gestalt principle of Closure to shape the perception of the form of the clusters, rather than use an explicit divisive encoding.

Multiple Route Choice Behaviour Analysis TBD'15

Min Lu, Chufan Lai, Tangzhi Ye, Jie Liang, and Xiaoru Yuan
Paper (1.2 MB)

Drivers make route choice everyday. This project studies the factors that influence drivers' route choice decision. Potential factors are extracted from general GPS trajectory dataset, such as travelling distance of each routes, route significance, departing time etc. The factors' impacts are visually studied as well as mathmetically evaluated.

Different routes have different traffic conditions. Even the same route, its traffic condition changes over time. Study of Traffic Jam Propagation among Road Segments VAST'13

Zuchao Wang, Min Lu, Xiaoru Yuan, Junping Zhang, and Huub van de Wetering
Paper (1.7 MB)

Traffic congestion is an urban issue which millions of people come across everyday. Understanding how the traffic congestion propagate is essential in transportation field. Based on taxi GPS trajectories, this project studies the variation of traffic conditions for each road, and the propagation of traffic jams along the road network.
